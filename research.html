Undergraduate Research

Throughout my undergraduate career I have been very fortunate to work on three different research projects at three different institutions. I broadly started in the field of computer science research and continued to narrow down my emphasis until I landed at computer science education.

  • {Computer Science Education - University of Washington - Advisor: Amy Ko}

    • There are many barriers that prevent a variety of students from entering the field of computer science. In order to better understand how to break down these barriers, I studied the social help-seeking behaviors of CS1 students. In particular, I conducted semi-structed interviews that aimed at learning the factors that influence when students seek social assistance and who they ask for help.

  • {Software Engineering - Carnegie Mellon University - Advisor: Joshua Sunshine}—I worked on the Penrose project, a platform which turns simple mathematical notation into diagrams. I implemented Penrose Tutor, which expands this system to teach math with visualizations. I also tackled the issue of how students were going to seamlessly begin using Penrose without having an understanding of the programming languages that run it. I designed tutoring systems that would guide students through this.
  • {Virtual Reality - Macalester College - Adivsor: Bret Jackson}—Within VR, I focused on 3D partial-object selection interfaces. I implemented two selection interfaces in C# with Unity using an HTC Vive head mounted VR display, wireless Vive controllers, and a laser-based tracking system. The project concluded with a pilot study that evaluated these two interfaces against a third.
