About

Academics

I am a senior at Macalester College majoring in computer science and applied mathematics and statistics with a concentration in cognitive science. I am expecting to graduate in May 2021. After graduation, I hope to keep conducting research and pursue a PhD in computer science education.

My desire to continue forward with academia has come about primarily from my positive experiences studying at a liberal arts college. I have had incredible mentors that have been consistently involved in my academic career and invested in my success as a student. Furthermore, I have had strong women role models within my majors that have supported my entrance into the field of computer science. I have also had the opportunity to be a teacher assistant for various computer science and statistics courses, which has introduced me to peer mentorship. A combination of all three of these factors has caused me to grow as an individual and a scientist. I developed a love of teaching and a desire to help others join this field. Computer science education is a relatively new domain that allows me to pursue all of my goals simultaneously, and I hope to use my research to support others who want to join.

Upbringing

While I have spent most of my life in Washington State, my upbringing involved a continuous blend of cultures, languages, and travel that originated with my family's history of moving around the globe. My dad was born in Argentina and moved to Israel at the age of 17. His parents were born in Poland, and migrated to Argentina at the start of the WWII to escape the Holocaust. My mom was raised in Israel, and was born on the ship that was bringing over her parents from Morocco around the foundation of Israel as a country. Soon after my parents met in Israel, they moved to Spain and had both my older brother and me. Two years after I was born, we settled in Redmond, WA for work purposes and fell in love with the area.

Due to the familial connection, I spent most of my childhood summers in Israel and visited Spain often. I am deeply grateful for the experience of traveling frequently and for ensuring I was conversational in both Hebrew and Spanish. With all that in mind, I truly have loved growing up in the Pacific Northwest. There is nothing quite like the constant greenery and the combination of forests, mountains, ocean, and city to provide the most breathtaking sights.

Extracurriculars

Outside of academia and research I have a passion for hiking, dancing, art, and service towards animals.

During those summers I spent in Israel, my family would often take camping trips to the north end of the country. This is where I developed my love for the outdoors, travel, and hiking. There is nothing more satisfying than witnessing the magnificent sights nature has to offer after a long day of walking.

Growing up, I spent many of my afternoons in the dance studio. I engaged with many different forms of dance, such as ballet, jazz, and one singular class of tap. The style that stuck with me the most was hip hop. By the time I hit high school I solely took hip hop classes and in college, I started my own hip hop dance group to continue participating in the dance form.

Along with dance classes, I spent most of my years during primary education taking art classes. I learned how to use a variety of mediums, such as acrylic paint, clay, oil and soft pastels, and pencil. After taking a small break when starting college, the extra free time I currently have due to the pandemic has brought me back to art. I mostly focus on acrylic paint and oil pastels.
